# Strategy

## 1. Project Overview

**Project name:** Jones Barber Shop
**Project type:** Static single-page marketing website
**Current stage:** Existing codebase — actively maintained, placeholder content present
**Primary purpose:** Establish an online presence for the shop, showcase services and barbers, and give prospective clients a way to inquire about bookings
**What this is not:** An e-commerce platform, a booking system with real-time availability, or a social media replacement

## 2. Mission / Purpose

> This project exists to give Jones Barber Shop a trusted, community-rooted online presence that converts neighborhood walk-in awareness into informed, loyal clients by presenting real services, real barbers, and real shop culture — with no invented claims.

## 3. Primary Goals

| Goal | Desired Outcome | Why It Matters | Success Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Visibility | Prospective clients find the shop online | The shop competes with chains that have digital presence | Traffic from local search |
| Service clarity | Visitors understand exactly what services are offered and at what price | Reduces friction at the door | Low confusion calls, high walk-in conversion |
| Barber trust | Clients feel they know who will cut their hair before arriving | Personal relationships are the shop's core product | Repeat bookings per barber |
| Booking inquiry | Site captures appointment requests before clients walk away | Reduces missed revenue from unanswered walk-ins | Form submissions |
| Community identity | The site feels like the neighborhood, not a chain | Differentiation from franchises | Brand recognition in Joneswood District |

## 4. Target Audiences

**Walk-in clients (primary)**
- Who: Local adults looking for a reliable neighborhood fade or cut
- Need: Know the shop is open, see prices, confirm it's legit
- Problem: Can't find the shop online or doesn't know what to expect
- Action: Visit in person or submit a booking inquiry
- Avoid messaging: Corporate, generic, chain-store language

**Regular customers**
- Who: Existing clients with a preferred barber
- Need: Quick access to hours, phone number, and booking
- Problem: No easy digital touchpoint between visits
- Action: Call, DM, or use the contact form
- Avoid messaging: Introductory content they already know

**Parents (kids cuts)**
- Who: Parents looking for a barber comfortable with children
- Need: Confidence the shop is kid-friendly and has a patient barber
- Problem: Uncertainty about environment and skill set
- Action: Book for a kids cut with Trey Collins
- Avoid messaging: Adult-only or intimidating barbershop tropes

**First-time visitors**
- Who: People new to the neighborhood or looking for a switch
- Need: Social proof, pricing, and clear services
- Problem: Don't know if the shop fits their hair type or culture
- Action: Read barber bios, check gallery, submit inquiry
- Avoid messaging: Assuming prior knowledge of the shop

## 5. Core Offering / Value Proposition

**Primary offering:** Precision cuts in a community barbershop — fades, tapers, line-ups, kids cuts, beard work, designs, waves maintenance, and loc maintenance

**Secondary offerings:**
- Four specialist barbers with distinct skill sets
- Black hair expertise
- 25+ years of community presence
- Walk-ins welcome

**Strongest differentiator:** Authentic neighborhood shop with deep cultural roots — not a chain, not a franchise

**Proof currently available:** 28-year operating history (since 1998), four barber profiles, 5-star testimonial cards

**Proof still missing:** Real photography (currently Pexels placeholders), real address, real phone number, verified testimonials

## 6. Content Strategy

- **Tone:** Direct, warm, confident — the voice of someone who has been cutting hair in the neighborhood for 28 years
- **Voice:** Authentic, community-first, no corporate polish
- **Reading level:** Plain language — 7th to 8th grade reading level
- **Key messages:** Sharp cuts. Real conversations. Community since 1998.
- **CTA strategy:** Two primary CTAs — "Book Now" (scroll to form) and phone number. Secondary: "Walk-Ins Welcome" badge
- **Content types:** Service descriptions, barber bios, pricing, hours, location, client testimonials
- **Metadata needs:** Title, description, and Open Graph tags (currently missing — flagged as roadmap item)
- **Do not claim:** Invented awards, unverified ratings counts, fabricated client numbers beyond "150+ weekly cuts" which is already in the site

## 7. Brand / Design Direction

- **Desired impression:** Established, trustworthy, culturally aware, cool without trying too hard
- **Visual personality:** Dark, cinematic, 90s soul — not trendy, not sterile
- **Current palette:** Background `#050509`, surface `#11111a`, elevated `#181824`; accents Red `#e0474c`, Blue `#3d6ee6`, Gold `#f5b544`, Off-white `#f5f1e8`
- **Design qualities to avoid:** Bright white corporate feel, generic stock-photo barbershop aesthetic, over-animated UI

## 8. Constraints

- **Stack:** Single HTML file — no build tool, no framework, no package manager
- **Budget:** Unknown — assume minimal
- **Hosting:** GitHub Pages candidate (free, static)
- **Images:** Pexels placeholders must be replaced with real shop photography before production launch
- **Legal:** Phone, address, and email are currently fictional placeholders
- **Accessibility:** Site should pass basic contrast and keyboard navigation expectations
- **Timeline:** Not specified — driven by owner availability

## 9. Strategic Non-Negotiables

- Do not invent metrics, testimonials, or capabilities not present in the actual site
- Preserve the 90s soul aesthetic and dark theme — do not genericize
- Keep the community-first voice — the shop's identity is its differentiator
- Placeholder content must be clearly marked and replaced with real info before launch
- No external libraries or dependencies without a plan-approved justification
